# M2.1 - Drone module (airborne relay + flight/link envelope)

A branch of **M2** (mapping & coverage). It reuses the same terrain (DEM), clutter, and propagation engine - a drone is just a comms node whose antenna height is its **altitude above ground (AGL)**. Read together with `M2-propagation.md`.

## Goal

Add drones / UAS to the comms plan in two ways:

- **A. Airborne relay / repeater** - put a relay up high to extend coverage.
- **B. Drone operating / link envelope** - from a position X, show where a drone can fly and keep its control + video link.

## A. Drone as airborne relay

- Model a transmitter at **altitude H above ground** over a point, an orbit, or a route. ITM already takes a tx height, so the only new thing is letting a node sit at drone altitude.
- Output: coverage from the airborne relay, plus the **gain vs a ground mast** at the same spot (height is the biggest lever in VHF/UHF).
- Add **"airborne relay"** as a relay option and as a **PACE path** (usually Alternate / Contingency - it extends reach but is time-limited).
- **Multi-hop:** ground node -> drone relay -> distant site / handheld, so a team in a valley reaches HQ via the drone. Chain the coverage.
- **Practical constraints to surface (not just physics):**
  - *Payload:* a full DMR repeater + duplexer + battery is heavy; a small multirotor may not lift it. Options: a light cross-band / relay payload, a larger UAS, or a **tethered drone** for a real repeater.
  - *Endurance / persistence:* multirotor on battery = tens of minutes of station-keeping; **tethered** (power + data down the tether) = persistent relay; VTOL / fixed-wing = longer loiter.
  - *Station-keeping:* hover vs orbit, wind, GPS hold.
  - *Power:* battery vs tether vs generator at the launch point.

## B. Drone operating / link envelope from a position

- From a ground station at **X** (operator / GCS), compute where the drone can fly and **keep its links**:
  - *C2 (command)* link and *video downlink* - each its own band / power.
  - Terrain **line-of-sight** from X at chosen **altitude bands** (e.g. 50 / 100 / 120 m AGL).
- Output: a **fly / link zone** layer + the terrain **shadow** areas where the link drops, and the **altitude needed** to clear terrain into a target area.
- Inputs: drone radio set (C2 freq, video freq / power), **GCS antenna height at X**, drone altitude band, optional relay (a drone relay from part A can extend the envelope).

## Kit hooks

- Platform: commercial (e.g. DJI Matrice-class) preferred for ops; a tethered rig for persistent relay.
- Video / BVLOS: **Starlink Mini** for streaming beyond RF video range; **ATAK / TAK** for the moving-map, the video feed, and the drone as a marker / sensor.
- Repeater payload: a **MOTOTRBO**-class repeater is heavy -> flag payload / endurance; prefer a light relay or a tether for a true repeater.

## Regulatory / ops overlays (later)

- VLOS / **BVLOS** distinction, max-altitude caps, no-fly / restricted-airspace overlays, observer placement. Planning-grade overlays - not authorisation.

## Reuse & phasing

- **Near-term:** airborne-relay coverage (a node at altitude) - the engine already supports it; add altitude to a node + a "gain vs ground" readout.
- **Then:** the flight / link envelope layer (LOS + link budget by altitude) and multi-hop chaining.
- **Later:** BVLOS / regulatory overlays, tethered-persistence modelling, and the 3D view (drone + coverage over 3D terrain).

## Acceptance criteria (M2.1)

1. A node can be placed at a drone **altitude AGL**; its coverage renders and shows the gain vs the same point on the ground.
1. "Airborne relay" appears as a relay / PACE option and can form a 2-hop chain to a target.
1. From a position X, a fly / link zone + terrain-shadow layer renders for a chosen altitude band.
1. Payload / endurance / tether caveats are shown so plans stay realistic.
